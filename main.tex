    which assigns to each natural transformation $\sigma:\cC(-,c)\Rightarrow K$ in $\cC\downarrow K$ the presheaf $\cC(-,c)$, and sends each morphism in $\cC\downarrow K$

    \end{tikzcd}\]
    to the arrow $f_\ast$ in $\Set_\cC$. Then $K$ is the colimit of $F$.
  \end{lemma}
  \begin{proof}
    In this proof, given an object $c$ in $\cC$, we will write $h_c$ to denote the representable functor $\cC(-,c)\in\Set_\cC$.

    By the universal property of the colimit, it suffices to show that for any presheaf $X$, there is a natural bijection
    \[\Set_\cC(K,X)\cong[\cC\downarrow K,\Set_\cC](F,\ul X).\]
    First, suppose we are given a natural transformation $\vare:F\Rightarrow\ul X$. Then for each $\sigma:h_c\to K$ in $\cC\downarrow K$, we have a natural transformation $\vare_\sigma:h_c\to X$. 

    First, define a cone $\eta$ under $F$ with nadir $K$ by defining $\eta_\sigma:\cC(-,c)\Rightarrow K$ to be simply $\sigma$. This clearly satisfies the naturality condition by how $\cC\downarrow K$ is defined. For objects $c$ in $\cC$, define maps $\lambda_c:\Set_\cC(\cC(-,c),K)\Rightarrow\Set_\cC(\cC(-,c),X)$ (here the diagrams are indexed by $c\in\cC$) to send $\alpha\mapsto\vare_\alpha$. We claim these maps are natural in $c$. Indeed, given $f:c\to c'$ in $\cC$, we want to show the following diagram commutes:
    \begin{equation}\label{DE1}\begin{tikzcd}
      {\Set_\cC(\cC(-,c'),K)} && {\Set_\cC(\cC(-,c'),X)} \\
      \\
      {\Set_\cC(\cC(-,c),K)} && {\Set_\cC(\cC(-,c),X)}
      \arrow["{\lambda_{c'}}", from=1-1, to=1-3]
      \arrow["{\Set_\cC(f_\ast,X)}", from=1-3, to=3-3]
      \arrow["{\Set_\cC(f_\ast,K)}"', from=1-1, to=3-1]
      \arrow["{\lambda_c}"', from=3-1, to=3-3]
    \end{tikzcd}\end{equation}
    Chasing an element $\alpha$ around the top of the diagram yields $\vare_\alpha\circ f_\ast$, while chasing $\alpha$ around the bottom of the diagram yields $\vare_{\alpha\circ f_\ast}$. Then by the naturality condition for $\vare$, the following diagram commutes:
